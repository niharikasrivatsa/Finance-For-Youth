Bonds

Bonds are investment securities where an investor lends money to a company or a government for a set period of time, in exchange for regular interest payments. Fixed income is a term often used to describe bonds, since your investment earns fixed payments over the life of the bond. Corporations and governments finance bonds to fund their operations.

Types of Bonds

Corporate Bonds are bonds issued by corporations to fund their ongoing operations. These type of bonds are low risk.

A Treasury Bill or a T-Bill is a short-term government debt obligation backed by the Treasury Department with a maturity of one year or less.

A junk bond is a high-yield bond, meaning that you will earn more money from it, but it is higher risk.
